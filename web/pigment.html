How this works

The page loads the self-contained mixbox.js (it embeds and decodes its own 512×512 lookup table). At init we call mixbox.lutTexture(gl) to upload the LUT and inject mixbox.glsl() into the fragment shader. Each pixel's blend factor t comes from its x position, and the shader calls mixbox_lerp(A, B, t) on the top half and mix(A, B, t) on the bottom. Nothing here is precomputed on the CPU — the pigment mixing runs per-fragment on the GPU, which is exactly the path a 3D voxel renderer would use.
