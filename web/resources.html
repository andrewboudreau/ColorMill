ColorMill Resources

This page collects the working vocabulary, visual references, open questions, and future resources we want available from the UI while designing the two-roll mill simulator.

Reference angle: the simulator is based on a mostly front-on view of the mill, looking at the visible band where material feeds into the roller nip and returns along the lower/front region.

Visual reference notes

  • The active mixing area is the narrow nip line/horizon where the two rollers press the material.
  • The top visible area is feed/entry into the nip, not a second mixer.
  • The bottom visible band is return/transport. It carries color state but should not continuously blur material like another nip.
  • The material should feel like a continuous sheet or curtain, not random fluid filling the entire screen.
  • Good visual behavior should produce elongated streaks, folding/banding behavior, and gradual homogenization over repeated passes.

Working vocabulary

TermDefinition
Two-roll millThe machine with two parallel rollers that pull, compress, sheet, and mix material.
RollerA rotating cylinder with diameter, width, RPM, surface speed, grip, and direction.
NipThe narrow gap where the rollers are closest. The primary active mixing event.
GapThe distance between rollers at the nip. Smaller gap means more compression and shear.
BandThe sheet of material carried by the rollers.
Feed zoneThe region where material approaches the nip.
Mixing horizonThe narrow simulated strip where diffusion/dispersion are allowed to be strong.
Return pathThe lower/front visible region where material travels after the nip.
PassOne traversal of material through the nip.
Cut/foldManual operation that layers or reorients material before another pass.
DispersionHow evenly pigment has been broken up and distributed through material.
ConcentrationPigment mass divided by material mass in a cell or region.

Simulation concepts

SimulationGrid

The numerical grid that stores the front-view state of the material band.

GridCell

A small patch of material state: material amount, pigment masses, velocity, and eventually thickness/dispersion.

BandMask

A 0..1 field defining where silicone is allowed to exist.

MixMask

A 0..1 field defining where active nip mixing is happening.

VelocityField

A grid of movement vectors generated from roller geometry and material path assumptions.

Renderer

Converts material/pigment state into visible color based on base material, opacity, and pigment concentration.

Open design questions

  1. What real mill size should the default simulator represent?
  2. Should the first scale target hobby/small-batch silicone, lab-size rubber mill, or an abstract educational mill?
  3. What default roller diameter, roller width, RPM, and gap range should we use?
  4. How should batch weight translate into grid material mass and thickness?
  5. How should translucent silicone render differently from opaque white silicone?
  6. How much mixing should happen from one pass through the nip?
  7. How should cut/fold operations be represented: direct grid remap, layer stack, or separate pass object?
  8. Should we represent left and right roller speeds separately to support speed ratio?
  9. What is the simplest useful definition of dispersion quality?
